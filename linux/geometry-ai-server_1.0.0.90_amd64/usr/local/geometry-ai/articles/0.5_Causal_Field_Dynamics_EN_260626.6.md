### §2.2　Definition of Transient Causal Advancing

**Definition (Transient Causal Advancing).** In the transient phase, causal advancing T_trans : Σ → Σ is a monotonic mapping toward the ground state along the η direction:

$$T_{\text{trans}}(\xi, \eta) = \left( e^{\Lambda_\xi} \xi, \eta + \delta\eta \right)$$

where δη = 1/√λ₁ is the bending structure quantization step size, and Λ_ξ = −½ log(λ₂/λ₁) < 0 is the contraction exponent of the hard mode direction (rigorously derived in Chapter 6).

**Proposition 2.1 (Monotonicity of Transient Advancing).** S(θ) decreases monotonically along the η direction, i.e., S(T_trans(θ)) ≤ S(θ), with equality only at the ground state θ*.

**Proof.** By 0.2.1 Theorem 2.4, S(θ) has a unique minimum on Σ. In this paper's (ξ,η) coordinate system, the gradient transformation of §2.1.1 gives ∂S/∂η|₀ = −1097.60 rad⁻¹ (at the bare benchmark point), which is negative — increasing η corresponds to approaching the ground state (η* = 60° > η₀ = 32.07°), and S decreases. The sign of this derivative is preserved over η ∈ [η₀, η*] by the strict convexity of S (0.2.1). Hence S decreases monotonically along the +η direction. ∎

> **Note (Gradient Value Provenance).** The value ∂S/∂η̃|₀ = −60.640710 rad⁻¹ given in 0.2.1 §4.3 is the partial derivative in the (ξ̃,η̃) coordinate system **with ξ̃ held fixed (i.e., θ_I held fixed)**. In this paper's (ξ,η) coordinate system, when ξ = θ_C − θ_I is held fixed while η varies, θ_I is not fixed (∂θ_I/∂η|_ξ = 1/2), hence the gradient transformation formula of §2.1.1 must be used. Both values share the same sign (both negative), not affecting the proposition's conclusion.

**Proposition 2.2 (Finite-Step Convergence).** Transient causal advancing reaches the neighborhood of the ground state in a finite number of steps. The number of steps N_cause = ⌈Δη / δη⌉, where Δη = η* − η₀.

**Proof.** By Proposition 2.1, each step increases η by δη. Total increment Δη = 60° − 32.07° = 27.93° = 0.487 rad. Unit step size δη = 1/√392.21 = 0.0505 rad = 2.89°. Hence N = Δη / δη ≈ 9.65, rounded up to 10. ∎

**Note.** Transient advancing T_trans is not measure-preserving (ξ direction contracts) and not ergodic (orbits converge monotonically to the ground state). Ergodic theory applies to the steady-state circulation described below.

### §2.3　Construction of Steady-State Causal Circulation

**Definition (Steady-State Causal Circulation).** On the central submanifold E^c = {ξ = 0} (the η-direction at the ground state θ*), define steady-state causal circulation T_ss : E^c → E^c as:

$$T_{\text{ss}}(0, \eta) = (0, \eta + \delta\eta \mod \Delta\eta_{\text{ss}})$$

where Δη_ss is the steady-state circulation period, corresponding to the total η variation over one complete circuit on E^c. Equivalently, on the excitation parameter space S¹ (0.1), T_ss is an irrational rotation φ ↦ φ + δφ, where δφ = 2π · δη / Δη_ss.

> **Honest Labeling.** The specific numerical value of Δη_ss depends on the embedding geometry of S¹ in the constraint section Σ, and its complete determination requires returning to the explicit form of the S¹ parameterization in 0.1. It is labeled here as a quantity to be determined. The ergodicity condition (δφ/2π = δη/Δη_ss ∉ ℚ) depends only on whether this ratio is irrational, not on the specific numerical value of Δη_ss.

**Proposition 2.3 (Measure-Preserving Property of Steady-State Circulation).** T_ss preserves the induced measure dμ_c = √λ₁ dη on E^c.

**Proof.** T_ss is a rigid translation, det(DT_ss) = 1. Hence μ_c(T_ss⁻¹(A)) = μ_c(A). ∎

## Chapter 3　Ergodic Theory Framework for Steady-State Causal Circulation

### §3.1　Measure-Preserving Transformation and Ergodicity

**Theorem 3.1 (Ergodicity).** The steady-state causal circulation (E^c, μ_c, T_ss) is ergodic; i.e., for any measurable set A ⊂ E^c, T_ss⁻¹(A) = A implies μ_c(A) = 0 or μ_c(A) = 1.

**Proof.** By Proposition 2.3, T_ss is measure-preserving. The theorem's validity depends on the following hypothesis:

> **Hypothesis 3.1 (Irrational Rotation Condition).** The ratio of the rotation step size to the period, δφ/2π = δη/Δη_ss, is an irrational number.

This hypothesis is motivated by the spectral rigidity of λ₁ and the irrationality expectations of the Dimensional Bridge, but a rigorous mathematical proof has not yet been given within the present framework. Under Hypothesis 3.1, the orbit of T_ss is dense in E^c. Hence no non-trivial invariant set exists: any positive-measure invariant set must contain a dense orbit and is thus dense in the whole space; being measurable, it must be either the whole space or a null set. ∎ (dependent on Hypothesis 3.1)

> **Honest Labeling.** The ergodicity conclusion of Theorem 3.1 strictly depends on the Irrational Rotation Condition (Hypothesis 3.1). This condition is physically natural within the current Geometric Theory framework (the spectral rigidity of λ₁ makes δη a geometric intrinsic quantity, and Δη_ss is determined by the S¹ embedding geometry; their ratio is expected to be irrational), but a rigorous mathematical proof has not yet been completed. This paper labels this condition as a hypothesis; if it is falsified in subsequent work, the ergodicity conclusion must be re-examined.

**Note.** Ergodicity strictly belongs to the steady-state circulation T_ss. Transient advancing T_trans is itself not ergodic, but its transient orbit enters E^c in a finite number of steps, after which the ergodicity of T_ss takes over the statistical behavior.

### §3.2　Birkhoff Ergodic Theorem and Covering Theorem

**Theorem 3.2 (Birkhoff Ergodic Theorem).** For any f ∈ L¹(E^c, μ_c), the time-average of the steady-state circulation equals the space-average almost everywhere:

$$\lim_{N \to \infty} \frac{1}{N} \sum_{n=0}^{N-1} f(T_{\text{ss}}^n x) = \int_{E^c} f \, \mathrm{d}\mu_c$$

**Proof.** By Theorem 3.1 (ergodicity, dependent on Hypothesis 3.1), directly apply the Birkhoff Ergodic Theorem. ∎

**Ergodic Form of the Covering Theorem.** Let f = χ_A be the characteristic function of the Information Field coverage layer, with A ⊂ E^c the set of information units on the holographic screen. Then Birkhoff's Theorem gives:

$$\lim_{N \to \infty} \frac{1}{N} \sum_{n=0}^{N-1} \chi_A(T_{\text{ss}}^n x) = \mu_c(A)$$

The left side is the average number of information units covered per layer of advancing in the steady state; the right side is the spatial proportion. The ergodic form of the Covering Theorem is: In the steady state, for each layer of causal depth advanced, the Information Field is covered by a proportion μ_c(A) of one layer.

### §3.3　Mixing and Information Coverage

**Definition (Mixing).** The steady-state causal circulation T_ss is mixing if, for any measurable sets A, B ⊂ E^c:

$$\lim_{n \to \infty} \mu_c(T_{\text{ss}}^{-n}A \cap B) = \mu_c(A)\mu_c(B)$$

**Theorem 3.3 (Mixing).** The steady-state causal circulation T_ss is mixing.

**Proof Sketch.** From the analytic framework of 0.2.1.1, jointly invoking the following two theorems:

- **0.2.1.1 Theorem 3.1 (Green Function Existence and Uniqueness).** On the compactified model Σ ≅ S², the Green function G(ξ,η; ξ',η') of the elliptic operator L[Φ] = Q exists and is unique, with the solution given by the Green function integral representation Φ = Φ₀ + ∫_Σ G Q dΣ'.
- **0.2.1.1 Theorem 4.1 (Super-Algebraic Decay).** The spherical harmonic expansion coefficients satisfy |C_l^m| ≤ C_N · l⁻ᴺ (for any N > 0). This means that the residual contribution of high-order angular modes (l > 7) under Bott periodicity truncation decays super-algebraically (l⁻ᴺ), and correlations between different advancing layers decay super-algebraically as the layer-number difference |n − n'| grows.

In the ergodic framework of the irrational rotation T_ss, super-algebraically decaying correlation functions ⟨f(T_ss^n x), g(x)⟩ − ⟨f⟩⟨g⟩ → 0 (as n → ∞) imply mixing. ∎

> **Honest Labeling 1 (Refined Green Function Citation).** 0.2.1.1 Theorem 3.1 proves the **existence, uniqueness, and regularity estimates** of the Green function; Theorem 4.1 proves the **super-algebraic decay** of the spherical harmonic expansion coefficients (not the direct exponential decay of Green function spatial separation). The above proof directly equates "super-algebraically decaying correlation functions" with the mixing condition. Strictly speaking, super-algebraic decay of spherical harmonic expansions guarantees the decay of L² correlation functions, while mixing requires asymptotic independence for arbitrary pairs of measurable sets — the equivalence between the two requires further measure-theoretic arguments (e.g., via the continuous part of the Koopman operator spectrum). This paper marks this derivation step as "physically valid but mathematically not fully closed," with Honest Labeling.

> **Honest Labeling 2 (Ergodicity Transmission).** The above correlation function decay argument holds naturally in the irrational-rotation ergodic framework (decay of correlation functions for translation transformations is guaranteed by uniform distribution of orbits). However, because the ergodicity of Theorem 3.1 itself depends on Hypothesis 3.1 (Irrational Rotation Condition), the mixing conclusion is likewise subject to this hypothesis.

**Physical Significance.** Mixing guarantees the uniformity of Information Field coverage — after sufficiently many steps of steady-state causal advancing, information units are uniformly distributed on the holographic screen, with no memory effect.

## Chapter 4　Dynamical Systems Formulation of Causal Time

### §4.1　One-Parameter Lie Group and Exponential Map

**Definition (Causal Generator).** The evolution of the Causal Field state C(θ; τ) is driven by the causal generator X:

$$X = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\lambda_1}} \frac{\partial}{\partial\eta}$$

Causal advancing is the action of the one-parameter Lie group exp(τ X): C(θ; τ) = e^{τ X} C(θ; 0).

**Theorem 4.1 (Lie Group Structure).** Causal advancing constitutes a one-parameter transformation group of ℝ on E^c, satisfying:

$$T_{\tau_1} \circ T_{\tau_2} = T_{\tau_1 + \tau_2}, \quad T_0 = \text{id}$$

**Proof.** By the additivity of causal time τ = √λ₁ · η, the advancing step size is δτ = √λ₁ · δη = 1. Hence T_τ is an additive group representation of ℝ. ∎

**Exponential Map.** exp(τ X) = ∑_{k=0}^∞ τ^k X^k / k!. Since X is a translation operator along the η direction, X^k = λ₁^{-k/2} ∂^k/∂η^k. On the space of analytic functions, the series converges.

### §4.2　Lyapunov Exponents and Hyperbolic Structure

**Definition (Lyapunov Exponent).** Under the action of transient advancing T_trans, the Lyapunov exponent of a tangent vector v ∈ T_θ Σ is:

$$\Lambda(v) = \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{1}{n} \log \|DT_{\text{trans}}^n v\|$$

**Theorem 4.2 (Lyapunov Spectrum).** The Lyapunov spectrum of transient causal advancing is {Λ₁, Λ₂} = {0, −½ log(λ₂/λ₁)}.
