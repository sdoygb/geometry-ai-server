### §7.1: Finite Resolution as an Ontological Principle

The "continuous" physics of the Standard Model is, in this picture, an effective description valid above the resolution limit.

### §7.2: Spectral Data Before Coordinate Geometry

The traditional approach to physics takes coordinate geometry as fundamental: spacetime is a manifold with a metric, fields are functions on this manifold, and dynamics are partial differential equations in coordinates.

The spectral upgrade inverts this hierarchy: **spectral data are ontologically prior to coordinate geometry.** The spectral triple $(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{H}, D, J, \gamma)$ contains all geometric information about the manifold $M(a)$ — by Connes' Reconstruction Theorem, the spectral data uniquely determine the diffeomorphism class of a spin manifold. Coordinates are a **derived** concept, useful for computation but not fundamental.

- Physical quantities are identified with spectral invariants (Wodzicki residue, Dixmier trace, heat kernel coefficients, spectral asymmetry), not with coordinate-dependent expressions;

The shift from particle parameterics to spectral geometric physics changes the meaning of "prediction":

- **Particle parameterics predicts:** "The mass of particle X is Y eV."
