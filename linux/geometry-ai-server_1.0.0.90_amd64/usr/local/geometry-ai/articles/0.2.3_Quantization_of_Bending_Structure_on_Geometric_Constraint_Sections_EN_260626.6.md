For $N = 2$, the joint constraint is $\theta_M^i + \theta_C^i + \theta_I^i = 90^\circ$ ($i = 1,2$). Introduce center-of-mass coordinates:

$$\theta_\alpha^{\text{cm}} = \frac{\theta_\alpha^1 + \theta_\alpha^2}{2}, \quad \Delta\theta_\alpha = \theta_\alpha^1 - \theta_\alpha^2$$

The center-of-mass coordinates satisfy $\sum_\alpha \theta_\alpha^{\text{cm}} = 90^\circ$; the relative coordinates satisfy $\sum_\alpha \Delta\theta_\alpha = 0$. The joint action decomposes as:

$$S_2 = S_{\text{cm}} + S_{\text{rel}}$$

$$S_{\text{cm}} = 2 \cdot S(\theta_M^{\text{cm}}, \theta_C^{\text{cm}}, \theta_I^{\text{cm}})$$

$$S_{\text{rel}} = S_{12}(\theta^{\text{cm}}, \Delta\theta)$$

The joint Hessian matrix in $(\theta^{\text{cm}}, \Delta\theta)$ coordinates has a block structure. At the stable equilibrium point $\Delta\theta_\alpha = 0$, $H_{\text{mix}} = 0$, and the Hessian is strictly block-diagonal.

### §4.3 Theorem-Level Derivation of Binding Energy

$$\Delta S = S_2^{\text{eff}} - 2S_1 = -\delta_{12} \cdot \frac{|\nabla S|}{3} < 0$$

$$\Delta S = S_{\text{cm}}^{\text{eff}} - S_1 = \nabla S \cdot \delta\theta^{\text{cm}}$$

where $\delta\theta^{\text{cm}} = (\delta_{12}/3, \delta_{12}/3, \delta_{12}/3)$ (uniform distribution along the normal). Hence

$$\Delta S = \frac{\delta_{12}}{3} \sum_\alpha \frac{\partial S}{\partial\theta_\alpha} = -\frac{\delta_{12}}{3} |\nabla S|$$

$$\boxed{E_{\text{bind}} = K \cdot |\Delta S| = K \cdot \frac{\delta_{12}}{3} \cdot |\nabla S|}$$

$$\boxed{\sum_{\alpha \in \{M,C,I\}} \theta_\alpha^{\text{cm, eff}} = 90^\circ + \delta_{12}}$$

$$\boxed{\delta_{12} = \hbar_{\text{eff}} \cdot S_e \cdot \sum_{\alpha} \frac{1}{\sin\theta_\alpha^{\text{cm}} \sqrt{H_{\alpha\alpha}^{(12)} \lambda_1}}}$$

**Step 1 (Non-additivity of mutual infiltration).** By 0.2.1.1, single-particle infiltration functions satisfy the sum identity $\sum_\alpha \Phi_\alpha^{(i)} = 0$ ($i = 1,2$). This identity ensures that the completeness constraint of a single particle is not broken by its own infiltration.

$$\sum_\alpha \Phi_\alpha^{(1 \to 2)} + \sum_\alpha \Phi_\alpha^{(2 \to 1)} = \delta_{12} \neq 0$$

where $\Phi_\alpha^{(i \to j)}$ is the effective angular correction to particle $j$ in the $\alpha$-direction due to the infiltration field of particle $i$.

**Step 2 (Hessian expression of mutual infiltration).** Mutual infiltration is mediated by the cross-coupling Hessian $H_{\alpha\beta}^{(12)}$. At the equilibrium point $\Delta\theta = 0$, $H_{\alpha\beta}^{(12)} = H_{\alpha\alpha}^{(12)} \delta_{\alpha\beta}$ (diagonally dominant). The infiltration correction from particle $j$ to particle $i$ is:

$$\Phi_\alpha^{(j \to i)} = \frac{\hbar_{\text{eff}}}{2} \cdot \frac{\partial}{\partial\theta_\alpha^i} \ln \det(H^{(12)} + H^{\text{cm}}) \Bigg|_{\text{eq}}$$

By 0.4 information field spectral graph theory, $\hbar_{\text{eff}} = 1/N_1$ is the effective Planck constant introduced by graph Laplacian discretization, characterizing the elementary scale of quantum fluctuations on the constraint section. $$\det(H^{\text{joint}}) = \det(H^{\text{cm}}) \cdot \det(H^{(12)})$$
