Since Theorem A.2 has already proved M² ≥ 0, the premise of Theorem 3.1 is automatically satisfied.

The Green function G(ξ, η; ξ′, η′) is defined as:

$$L_{\xi,\eta}[G] = \delta(\xi-\xi', \eta-\eta') - \frac{1}{A_\Sigma}$$

(the constant term is subtracted from the right-hand side to ensure zero-mean solvability). The integral representation of the solution is:

$$\Phi(\xi,\eta) = \Phi_0 + \int_\Sigma G(\xi,\eta; \xi',\eta') \, Q(\xi',\eta') \, d\Sigma'$$

## Chapter 4: Spherical Harmonic Expansion and Convergence

### §4.1 Orthogonal Basis on S²

The holographic screen Σ, after compactification, is homeomorphic to S² (Theorem C.1). $$\int_{S^2_R} Y_l^m(\vartheta,\varphi) \, Y_{l'}^{m'}(\vartheta,\varphi) \, d\Omega_R = \delta_{ll'} \, \delta_{mm'}$$

Pulling back via χ*, we obtain a complete orthogonal basis Ỹ_l^m = χ* Y_l^m of L²(Σ). Expand the Infiltration Function Φ as:

$$\Phi(\xi,\eta) = \sum_{l=0}^\infty \sum_{m=-l}^l C_l^m \, \widetilde Y_l^m(\xi,\eta)$$

### §4.2 Coefficient Decay Theorem

**Theorem 4.1**. If the source term Q ∈ C^∞(Σ), then the spherical harmonic expansion coefficients C_l^m possess super-algebraic decay rate:

For any N > 0, there exists a constant C_N > 0 such that |C_l^m| ≤ C_N · l^{-N}.

**Proof**. By the regularity estimate of §3.3, Φ ∈ C^∞(Σ). The spherical harmonic expansion coefficients of a smooth function on S² satisfy super-algebraic decay; this is a standard result in harmonic analysis (Stein-Weiss [15]). Specifically, for any k ∈ ℕ, Δ^k Φ ∈ L²(Σ), and the spherical harmonics are eigenfunctions of the Laplace-Beltrami operator: Δ Ỹ_l^m = −(l(l+1)/R²) Ỹ_l^m. Therefore:
$$C_l^m = \langle \Phi, \widetilde Y_l^m \rangle = \frac{(-1)^k R^{2k}}{[l(l+1)]^k} \cdot \langle \Delta^k \Phi, \widetilde Y_l^m \rangle$$
By Cauchy-Schwarz:
$$|C_l^m| \leq \frac{R^{2k}\|\Delta^k \Phi\|_{L^2}}{[l(l+1)]^k} \leq C_k \cdot l^{-2k}$$
For any N, take k > N/2 to obtain |C_l^m| ≤ C_N · l^{-N}. □

The super-algebraic decay rate of Theorem 4.1 depends only on elliptic regularity theory and the spectral geometry of S²; the universality of its decay exponent N does not depend on the specific calibrated values of higher-order curvature corrections.
