## Chapter 3　Representation-Theoretic Classification of the 230 Space Groups

### §3.1　Irreducible Representations of Point Groups

**Proposition 3.1 (Point Group Representation-Theoretic Compatibility):** The information field spherical harmonic modes on the geometric constraint section $\Sigma \cong S^2$ are compatible with the irreducible representations of the 32 three-dimensional crystallographic point groups.

**Argument:** The information field modes on the holographic screen $\Sigma \cong S^2$ are expanded in spherical harmonics $Y_l^m$. Under $SO(3)$ rotations, spherical harmonics transform according to irreducible representations $D^{(l)}$ (dimension $2l+1$). The 32 crystallographic point groups are discrete subgroups of $SO(3)$. By Frobenius reciprocity, the $SO(3)$ representation $D^{(l)}$ decomposes under the point group $G$ into a direct sum of irreducible representations of $G$:
$$D^{(l)}|_G = \bigoplus_\alpha n_\alpha^{(l)} D^{(\alpha)}$$
where $n_\alpha^{(l)}$ is the multiplicity of the irreducible representation $D^{(\alpha)}$, determined by the character formula:
$$n_\alpha^{(l)} = \frac{1}{|G|} \sum_{g \in G} \chi^{(l)}(g)^* \chi^{(\alpha)}(g)$$
The decompositions of low-order spherical harmonics ($l=0,1,2$) naturally cover the 32 point groups: $l=0$ ($D^{(0)}$, 1-dim) → totally symmetric representation $A_{1g}$; $l=1$ ($D^{(1)}$, 3-dim) → decomposes as $A_{2u} \oplus E_u$ (cubic groups) or $A_{2u} \oplus E_{1u}$ (hexagonal groups); $l=2$ ($D^{(2)}$, 5-dim) → decomposes as $E_g \oplus T_{2g}$ (cubic groups) or $E_{1g} \oplus E_{2g}$ (hexagonal groups).

> **Honest Labeling:** This proposition does not provide complete character tables exhaustively verifying all 32 point groups; it provides only the general framework of Frobenius reciprocity. "Compatible" means the representation-theoretic structure of spherical harmonic decompositions can accommodate all 32 point groups — not a rigorous one-to-one correspondence proof.

### §3.2　Projective Representations of Space Groups

**Proposition 3.2 (Projective Representations of Space Groups):** The 230 space groups, as discrete subgroups of the Euclidean group $E(3)$, have projective representations determined jointly by point group representations and the Bloch theorem for the translation group.

**Argument:** Elements of a space group $S$ are of the form $\{R|\mathbf{t}\}$, where $R \in G$ (point group) and $\mathbf{t}$ is a translation. By the Seitz representation, the space group acts on Hilbert space as:
$$\hat{P}(R|\mathbf{t})\psi(\mathbf{r}) = \psi(R^{-1}\mathbf{r} - R^{-1}\mathbf{t})$$
By Bloch's theorem, the irreducible representations of the translation subgroup $T$ are $\exp(i\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{t})$; hence the space group representations are reducible to representations of the point group at the "little group" of point $\mathbf{k}$. The complete classification of the 230 space groups is accomplished by the induced representations of little group representations.

> **Honest Labeling:** This is a framework-level description, not a rigorous, exhaustive classification proof for each of the 230 space groups. A complete classification requires computing little group representations and induced representations for all 230 space groups individually, which lies beyond the scope of this paper.

### §3.3　Geometric Constraints on Coordination Numbers

**Proposition 3.3 (Geometric Constraints on Coordination Numbers):** The coordination number (CN) is constrained by the irreducible representation dimensions of spherical harmonics on the holographic screen $S^2$ under point group action. The irreducible decompositions of low-order modes ($l \leq 2$) provide upper-bound estimates for the number of independent geometric directions.

The coordination number describes the number of nearest-neighbor interactions of a central atom and is a crystallographic geometric parameter. On $S^2$, low-order spherical harmonic modes correspond to fundamental geometric directions: $l=0$ ($D^{(0)}$, 1-dim): 1 totally symmetric direction; $l=1$ ($D^{(1)}$, 3-dim): 3 independent spatial directions; $l=2$ ($D^{(2)}$, 5-dim): 5 independent quadrupolar directions. Crystallographic point group symmetry further reduces the number of independent directions. This framework gives a qualitative constraint relation between geometric mode number and coordination number — not a quantitative equality.

### §4.2　Representation-Theoretic Selection Rules

**Proposition 4.1 (Representation-Theoretic Selection Rules for Orbital Activity):** Under point group $G$, atomic orbitals classify according to irreducible representations $D^{(\alpha)}$. The orbital degeneracy $N^{\mathrm{orb}}$ is the dimension $d_\alpha$ of the irreducible representation.
