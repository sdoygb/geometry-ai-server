## 2 Constrained Product Spheres and Spectral Rigidity

*Proof.* A faithful permutation group on three objects must be a subgroup of $S_3$. Transitivity requires orbit size 3, so $|G| \geq 3$. If transpositions (odd permutations) are required, then $A_3$ is insufficient, and the only possibility is $S_3$. $\square$

*Proof.* (1) If the three sectors are metrically equivalent, then any transposition (e.g., $\mathcal{M}\leftrightarrow\mathcal{C}$) preserves the product metric and thus induces an isometric automorphism. Hence all pairwise transpositions belong to $G$.
