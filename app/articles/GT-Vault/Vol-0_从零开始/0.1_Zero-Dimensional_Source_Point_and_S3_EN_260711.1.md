**Intuitive analogy:** Imagine a digital image. ## 0.1.5 Spectral Level Spaces

### Homogeneous Spaces

Each level of the Spectral Unfolding requires a mathematical object to carry its independent components. The natural carrier is the **homogeneous space** corresponding to the relevant subgroup.

**Homogeneous Space (GT-0.1.0.5)** Let $G$ be a group and $H \leq G$ a subgroup. The **homogeneous space** $G/H$ of $G$ with respect to $H$ is the coset space of the left-multiplication action of $H$ on $G$:

$$
G/H := \{gH \mid g \in G\}.
$$

The elements of $G/H$ are the left cosets of $H$. When $H$ is a normal subgroup, $G/H$ itself forms a group (the quotient group).

**Important distinction:**
- If $H \lhd G$ (normal), then $G/H$ is a quotient group, possessing a group structure
- If $H$ is not a normal subgroup, then $G/H$ is merely a homogeneous space (no group structure)

In the three-level situation of interest, $H_0 = \{e\}$ and $H_2 = S_3$ are normal subgroups (trivially), and $H_1 = A_3$ is also a normal subgroup. Hence all three homogeneous spaces naturally possess group structures, but we do not need the group structure — we only need their **coset counts**.

### Explicit Construction of the Three Homogeneous Spaces

**1. Coarsest level: $G/H_2 = S_3/S_3$**

$$
S_3/S_3 = \{S_3\}
$$

Only one coset — the entire group itself. Coset count: 1.

**2. Intermediate level: $G/H_1 = S_3/A_3$**

$A_3$ consists of the 3 even permutations: $\{e, \sigma, \sigma^2\}$. In $S_3$, even and odd permutations each account for half:

- Even-permutation coset: $A_3 = \{e, \sigma, \sigma^2\}$
- Odd-permutation coset: $\tau_{12}A_3 = \{\tau_{12}, \tau_{12}\sigma, \tau_{12}\sigma^2\} = \{\tau_{12}, \tau_{23}, \tau_{13}\}$

Thus $S_3/A_3 = \{A_3, \tau_{12}A_3\}$, with exactly 2 cosets. This corresponds to the even-odd dichotomy.

**Verification:**
$$
\tau_{12}A_3 = \{\tau_{12}e, \tau_{12}\sigma, \tau_{12}\sigma^2\} = \{\tau_{12}, \tau_{23}, \tau_{13}\},
$$
$$
\tau_{13}A_3 = \{\tau_{13}e, \tau_{13}\sigma, \tau_{13}\sigma^2\} = \{\tau_{13}, \tau_{12}, \tau_{23}\} = \tau_{12}A_3,
$$

so $\tau_{12}A_3 = \tau_{13}A_3 = \tau_{23}A_3$ — the three odd permutations belong to the same coset. Coset count is indeed 2.

**3. Finest level: $G/H_0 = S_3/\{e\}$**

Each left coset of $\{e\}$ contains exactly one element: $g\{e\} = \{g\}$. Hence

$$
S_3/\{e\} = \{\{e\}, \{\tau_{12}\}, \{\tau_{13}\}, \{\tau_{23}\}, \{\sigma\}, \{\sigma^2\}\},
$$

with coset count 6 — equal to the number of elements of $S_3$.

### Definition of Spectral Level Spaces

**Spectral Level Space (GT-0.1.0.6)** For each subgroup $H_i$ in the normal subgroup chain, define its **Spectral Level Space** as the space of complex-valued functions on the homogeneous space $G/H_i$:

$$
\mathcal H_i := L^2(G/H_i, \mathbb C).
$$

For a finite group $G$, $G/H_i$ is a finite set, so $L^2(G/H_i, \mathbb C)$ is a finite-dimensional complex vector space whose dimension equals the number of elements of $G/H_i$.

**Dimension of Spectral Level Spaces (GT-0.1.0.7)** The dimension of the Spectral Level Space $\mathcal H_i = L^2(G/H_i, \mathbb C)$ equals the index of the subgroup $H_i$ in $G$:

$$
\dim_{\mathbb C} \mathcal H_i = [G : H_i].
$$

*Proof.* Let the coset count of $G/H_i$ be $m = [G : H_i]$. Label the cosets as $C_1, C_2, \dots, C_m$. For each coset $C_j$, define the indicator function

$$
\delta_{C_j}(x) = \begin{cases}
1, & x \in C_j, \\[2pt]
0, & x \notin C_j.
\end{cases}
$$

These $\{\delta_{C_j}\}_{j=1}^m$ are vectors in $L^2(G/H_i)$, and:

- **Linear independence**: If $\sum_{j=1}^m a_j \delta_{C_j} = 0$ as the identically zero function, then evaluating on coset $C_k$ yields $a_k = 0$. Hence all coefficients are zero.
- **Spanning property**: Any function $f: G/H_i \to \mathbb C$ can be uniquely written as $f = \sum_{j=1}^m f(C_j) \delta_{C_j}$.

Thus $\{\delta_{C_j}\}_{j=1}^m$ forms a basis of $\mathcal H_i$, with cardinality $m = [G : H_i]$. By linear algebra, the dimension of a vector space equals the cardinality of any of its bases. Hence

$$
\dim \mathcal H_i = [G : H_i].
$$

∎

**Note:** The proof of this proposition uses no special properties of $S_3$ whatsoever — it holds for any finite group $G$ and any subgroup $H$. This is a universal relation between group actions and function spaces.

### Dimensions of the Three Spectral Level Spaces

Applying GT-0.1.0.7 to the three-level normal subgroup chain of $S_3$, using the indices already computed in §0.1.2:

**Level 2 (coarsest, $H_2 = S_3$):**
$$
\dim \mathcal H_2 = [S_3 : S_3] = \frac{6}{6} = 1.
$$

**Level 1 (intermediate, $H_1 = A_3$):**
$$
\dim \mathcal H_1 = [S_3 : A_3] = \frac{6}{3} = 2.
$$

**Level 0 (finest, $H_0 = \{e\}$):**
$$
\dim \mathcal H_0 = [S_3 : \{e\}] = \frac{6}{1} = 6.
$$

### Explicit Bases of the Spectral Level Spaces

As a cross-check, we explicitly construct the bases of the three Spectral Level Spaces to confirm the dimensions.

**$\mathcal H_2 = L^2(S_3/S_3)$:** The homogeneous space has a single point $S_3$. The function space is

$$
where $\delta_{S_3}$ is the function taking value 1 on the unique coset. $\dim \mathcal H_2 = 1$. Basis: $\{\delta_{S_3}\}$.

**$\mathcal H_1 = L^2(S_3/A_3)$:** The homogeneous space has two points $\{A_3, \tau_{12}A_3\}$. The function space is

$\dim \mathcal H_1 = 2$. Basis: $\{\delta_{A_3}, \delta_{\tau_{12}A_3}\}$.

$\dim \mathcal H_0 = 6$. Basis: $\{\delta_{\{e\}}, \delta_{\{\tau_{12}\}}, \delta_{\{\tau_{13}\}}, \delta_{\{\tau_{23}\}}, \delta_{\{\sigma\}}, \delta_{\{\sigma^2\}}\}$.

The dimensions of the three spaces are $1, 2, 6$, respectively, consistent with the computation of GT-0.1.0.7.

## 0.1.6 Spectral Weight

### Motivation for Spectral Weight

We now have three Spectral Level Spaces $\mathcal H_2, \mathcal H_1, \mathcal H_0$, of dimensions $1, 2, 6$ respectively. But what is the significance of dimension?

Recall the setup of the Zero-Dimensional Source Point: it is an "un-unfolded" point, with no preferred direction and no bias. When the Spectral Unfolding projects its symmetry onto three levels, the "independent information capacity" that each level can carry is determined by the dimension of that level's space.

Under the premise of the Zero-Dimensional Source Point being "unbiased," each level should receive a statistical weight proportional to its capacity to carry independent information. This weight is precisely the dimension of the Spectral Level Space.

**Why dimension?** Consider an analogy: suppose an unknown function is defined on $S_3$. If we can only observe this function at the $i$-th resolution level, then at most we can distinguish $\dim \mathcal H_i$ independent numerical values (because the dimension of $\mathcal H_i$ equals the number of independently variable directions at that resolution). In the absence of additional information, the "information capacity" at each resolution is the dimension of that space.

### Spectral Weight (GT-0.1.0.8)

In the Spectral Unfolding, the **Spectral Weight** $w_i$ of the level corresponding to subgroup $H_i$ is defined as the dimension of the Spectral Level Space $\mathcal H_i$:

$$
w_i := \dim_{\mathbb C} \mathcal H_i = [G : H_i].
$$

**Normalization:** Sometimes we discuss relative weights, normalizing the sum of all weights to unity:

$$
W_i = \frac{w_i}{\sum_{j=0}^{2} w_j}.
$$

The relative weights give the statistical proportion of each level.

### Spectral Weight Ratio (GT-0.1.0.9)

Under $S_3$ symmetry, the three-level spectral weight ratio of the Spectral Unfolding is uniquely determined as

$$
\boxed{w_0 : w_1 : w_2 = 6 : 2 : 1}.
$$

*Proof.* The proof proceeds in three steps, each with a clear mathematical basis.

**Step 1: Determine the three-level normal subgroup chain.**

The complete set of normal subgroups of $S_3$ is $\{e\}$, $A_3$, and $S_3$ (proved in §0.1.2). Hence the unique non-trivial normal subgroup chain is

$$
\{e\} \lhd A_3 \lhd S_3.
$$

The subgroups at the three levels are:
- $H_0 = \{e\}$ (finest level, trivial subgroup)
- $H_1 = A_3$ (intermediate level, alternating subgroup)
- $H_2 = S_3$ (coarsest level, whole group)

This is the only possible unfolding path — it is not an artificial choice, but is forced by the group-theoretic structure of $S_3$.

**Step 2: Compute the index at each level.**

By Lagrange's theorem: for a finite group $G$ and subgroup $H$, the index is

$$
[G : H] = \frac{|G|}{|H|}.
$$

Computing level by level:

| Level i | Subgroup Hᵢ | |Hᵢ| | |S₃|/|Hᵢ| | Index [S₃:Hᵢ] |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| 0 | {e} | 1 | 6/1 | 6 |
| 1 | A₃ | 3 | 6/3 | 2 |
| 2 | S₃ | 6 | 6/6 | 1 |

**Step 3: Obtain the ratio from the definition of Spectral Weight.**

By GT-0.1.0.8, the Spectral Weight $w_i = [S_3 : H_i]$. Hence

$$
w_0 = [S_3 : \{e\}] = 6,
$$
$$
w_1 = [S_3 : A_3] = 2,
$$
$$
w_2 = [S_3 : S_3] = 1.
$$

All the above computations hold exactly within the integers. Therefore the Spectral Weight ratio is

$$
w_0 : w_1 : w_2 = 6 : 2 : 1.
$$

∎

**Structural commentary on GT-0.1.0.9:**

The mathematical significance of the three weights is respectively:

- **$w_0 = 6$** (maximal unfolding level): corresponds to the trivial subgroup $\{e\}$. At this level all group elements are distinguished; the Spectral Level Space has $6$ independent directions. This is the finest resolution, carrying the richest spectral structure.

- **$w_1 = 2$** (intermediate level): corresponds to the alternating subgroup $A_3$. The even-odd dichotomy compresses 6 elements into 2 cosets; the Spectral Level Space has $2$ independent directions (even domain and odd domain).

- **$w_2 = 1$** (ground level): corresponds to the whole group $S_3$ itself. No distinctions whatsoever; the Spectral Level Space has only $1$ direction — a uniform background.

### Clarification on "Self-Evidence"

The proof of the Spectral Weight Ratio (GT-0.1.0.9) may appear simple (only 3 divisions), but each step relies on solid group-theoretic groundwork laid earlier:
- The **uniqueness** of the normal subgroup chain requires exhaustively listing all subgroups of $S_3$ and verifying normality (§0.1.2)

A short proof does not equal a leap — it merely front-loads the complexity into the preparatory work of §0.1.2 and §0.1.5.

3. **Geometrizing "existence" itself:** The Zero-Dimensional Source Point $\mathcal Z$ is posited to carry symmetry $S_3$, but is $\mathcal Z$ itself a starting point internal to the geometric theory, or a choice at the meta-theoretic level? This is a question requiring further argumentation.
