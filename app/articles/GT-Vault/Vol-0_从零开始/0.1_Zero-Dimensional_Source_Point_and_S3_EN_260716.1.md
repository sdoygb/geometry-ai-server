## 0.1.4 Spectral Unfolding

**Intuitive analogy:** Imagine a digital image. More precisely, spectral unfolding is defined as the mapping from the chain $\{G/H_i\}_{i=0}^{2}$ to the family of function spaces $\{L^2(G/H_i)\}_{i=0}^{2}$. Each level $i$ captures the spectral information resolvable at resolution $H_i$.

## 0.1.5 Spectral Hierarchy Spaces

### Homogeneous Spaces

Each level of the spectral unfolding requires a mathematical object to carry its independent components. The natural carrier is the **homogeneous space** corresponding to the relevant subgroup.

**Homogeneous Space (GT-0.1.0.5)** Let $G$ be a group and $H \leq G$ a subgroup. The **homogeneous space** of $G$ with respect to $H$, denoted $G/H$, is the coset space under the left-multiplication action of $H$ on $G$:

$$
G/H := \{gH \mid g \in G\}.
$$

The elements of $G/H$ are the left cosets of $H$. When $H$ is a normal subgroup, $G/H$ itself forms a group (the quotient group).

**Important distinction:**
- If $H \lhd G$ (normal), then $G/H$ is a quotient group with a group structure
- If $H$ is not a normal subgroup, then $G/H$ is merely a homogeneous space (without a group structure)

In the three-level case of our interest, $H_0 = \{e\}$ and $H_2 = S_3$ are normal subgroups (trivially), and $H_1 = A_3$ is also a normal subgroup. Hence all three homogeneous spaces naturally possess group structures, but we do not need the group structure—we only need their **coset counts**.

### Explicit Construction of the Three Homogeneous Spaces

**1. Coarsest level: $G/H_2 = S_3/S_3$**

$$
S_3/S_3 = \{S_3\}
$$

A single coset—the whole group itself. Coset count = 1.

**2. Intermediate level: $G/H_1 = S_3/A_3$**

$A_3$ contains 3 even permutations: $\{e, \sigma, \sigma^2\}$. In $S_3$, even and odd permutations each constitute half:

- Even coset: $A_3 = \{e, \sigma, \sigma^2\}$
- Odd coset: $\tau_{12}A_3 = \{\tau_{12}, \tau_{12}\sigma, \tau_{12}\sigma^2\} = \{\tau_{12}, \tau_{23}, \tau_{13}\}$

Thus $S_3/A_3 = \{A_3, \tau_{12}A_3\}$, with exactly 2 cosets. This corresponds to the even-odd dichotomy.

**Verification:**
$$
\tau_{12}A_3 = \{\tau_{12}e, \tau_{12}\sigma, \tau_{12}\sigma^2\} = \{\tau_{12}, \tau_{23}, \tau_{13}\},
$$
$$
\tau_{13}A_3 = \{\tau_{13}e, \tau_{13}\sigma, \tau_{13}\sigma^2\} = \{\tau_{13}, \tau_{12}, \tau_{23}\} = \tau_{12}A_3,
$$

So $\tau_{12}A_3 = \tau_{13}A_3 = \tau_{23}A_3$—all three odd permutations belong to the same coset. The coset count is indeed 2.

**3. Finest level: $G/H_0 = S_3/\{e\}$**

Each left coset of $\{e\}$ contains exactly one element: $g\{e\} = \{g\}$. Hence

$$
S_3/\{e\} = \{\{e\}, \{\tau_{12}\}, \{\tau_{13}\}, \{\tau_{23}\}, \{\sigma\}, \{\sigma^2\}\},
$$

Coset count = 6—equal to the number of elements in $S_3$.

### Definition of Spectral Hierarchy Spaces

**Spectral Hierarchy Space (GT-0.1.0.6)** For each subgroup $H_i$ in the normal subgroup chain, define its **spectral hierarchy space** as the space of complex-valued functions on the homogeneous space $G/H_i$:

$$
\mathcal{H}_i := L^2(G/H_i, \mathbb{C}).
$$

For a finite group $G$, $G/H_i$ is a finite set, hence $L^2(G/H_i, \mathbb{C})$ is a finite-dimensional complex vector space whose dimension equals the number of elements in $G/H_i$.

**Dimension of Spectral Hierarchy Spaces (GT-0.1.0.7)** The dimension of the spectral hierarchy space $\mathcal{H}_i = L^2(G/H_i, \mathbb{C})$ equals the index of the subgroup $H_i$ in $G$:

$$
\dim_{\mathbb{C}} \mathcal{H}_i = [G : H_i].
$$

*Proof.* Let the number of cosets of $H_i$ in $G$ be $m = [G : H_i]$. Label the cosets as $C_1, C_2, \dots, C_m$. For each coset $C_j$, define the indicator function

$$
\delta_{C_j}(x) = \begin{cases}
1, & x \in C_j, \\[2pt]
0, & x \notin C_j.
\end{cases}
$$

These $\{\delta_{C_j}\}_{j=1}^m$ are vectors in $L^2(G/H_i)$, and:

- **Linear independence**: If $\sum_{j=1}^m a_j \delta_{C_j} = 0$ is the identically zero function, then evaluating on coset $C_k$ yields $a_k = 0$. Hence all coefficients are zero.
- **Spanning property**: Any function $f: G/H_i \to \mathbb{C}$ can be uniquely written as $f = \sum_{j=1}^m f(C_j) \delta_{C_j}$.

Thus $\{\delta_{C_j}\}_{j=1}^m$ forms a basis of $\mathcal{H}_i$, and the cardinality of the basis is $m = [G : H_i]$. By linear algebra, the dimension of a vector space equals the cardinality of any one of its bases. Therefore

$$
\dim \mathcal{H}_i = [G : H_i].
$$

∎

**Note:** The proof of this proposition does not use any special property of $S_3$—it holds for any finite group $G$ and any subgroup $H$. This is a universal relation between group actions and function spaces.

### Dimensions of the Three Spectral Hierarchy Spaces

Applying GT-0.1.0.7 to the three-level normal subgroup chain of $S_3$, combined with the indices already computed in §0.1.2:

**Level 2 (coarsest layer, $H_2 = S_3$):**
$$
\dim \mathcal{H}_2 = [S_3 : S_3] = \frac{6}{6} = 1.
$$

**Level 1 (intermediate layer, $H_1 = A_3$):**
$$
\dim \mathcal{H}_1 = [S_3 : A_3] = \frac{6}{3} = 2.
$$

**Level 0 (finest layer, $H_0 = \{e\}$):**
$$
\dim \mathcal{H}_0 = [S_3 : \{e\}] = \frac{6}{1} = 6.
$$

### Explicit Bases of the Spectral Hierarchy Spaces

As a cross-check, we explicitly construct the bases of the three spectral hierarchy spaces to confirm the dimensions.

**$\mathcal{H}_2 = L^2(S_3/S_3)$:** The homogeneous space has exactly one point $S_3$. The function space is
$$
\mathcal{H}_2 = \mathbb{C} \cdot \delta_{S_3},
$$
where $\delta_{S_3}$ is the function taking value 1 on the unique coset. $\dim \mathcal{H}_2 = 1$. Basis: $\{\delta_{S_3}\}$.

**$\mathcal{H}_1 = L^2(S_3/A_3)$:** The homogeneous space has two points $\{A_3, \tau_{12}A_3\}$. The function space is
$$
\mathcal{H}_1 = \{a \delta_{A_3} + b \delta_{\tau_{12}A_3} \mid a, b \in \mathbb{C}\}.
$$
$\dim \mathcal{H}_1 = 2$. Basis: $\{\delta_{A_3}, \delta_{\tau_{12}A_3}\}$.

**$\mathcal{H}_0 = L^2(S_3/\{e\})$:** The homogeneous space has 6 points, corresponding to the 6 group elements. The function space is
$$
\mathcal{H}_0 = \left\{\sum_{g \in S_3} a_g \delta_{\{g\}} \;\middle|\; a_g \in \mathbb{C}\right\}.
$$
$\dim \mathcal{H}_0 = 6$. Basis: $\{\delta_{\{e\}}, \delta_{\{\tau_{12}\}}, \delta_{\{\tau_{13}\}}, \delta_{\{\tau_{23}\}}, \delta_{\{\sigma\}}, \delta_{\{\sigma^2\}}\}$.

The dimensions of the three spaces are $1, 2, 6$ respectively, consistent with the computation via GT-0.1.0.7.

## 0.1.6 Spectral Weights

### Motivation for Spectral Weights

We now have three spectral hierarchy spaces $\mathcal{H}_2, \mathcal{H}_1, \mathcal{H}_0$, of dimensions $1, 2, 6$ respectively. But what is the significance of the dimensions?

Recall the setup of the Zero-Dimensional Source Point: it is an "un-unfolded" point, with no preferred direction and no bias. When spectral unfolding projects its symmetry onto three levels, the "amount of independent information" that each level can carry is determined by the dimension of that level's space.

Under the premise of "no bias" for the Zero-Dimensional Source Point, each level should receive a statistical weight in accordance with its capacity to carry independent information. This weight is the dimension of the spectral hierarchy space.

**Why the dimension?** Consider an analogy: suppose an unknown function is defined on $S_3$. If we can only observe this function at the $i$-th resolution level, then at most we can resolve $\dim \mathcal{H}_i$ independent numerical values (because the dimension of $\mathcal{H}_i$ equals the number of independently variable directions at that resolution). In the absence of additional information, the "information capacity" at each resolution is precisely the dimension of the corresponding space.

### Spectral Weight (GT-0.1.0.8)

In the spectral unfolding, the **spectral weight** $w_i$ of the level corresponding to subgroup $H_i$ is defined as the dimension of the spectral hierarchy space $\mathcal{H}_i$:

$$
w_i := \dim_{\mathbb{C}} \mathcal{H}_i = [G : H_i].
$$

**Normalization:** Sometimes we discuss relative weights, normalizing the sum of all weights:

$$
W_i = \frac{w_i}{\sum_{j=0}^{2} w_j}.
$$

The relative weights give the statistical proportion of each level.

### Spectral Weight Ratio (GT-0.1.0.9)

Under $S_3$ symmetry, the three-level spectral weight ratio of the spectral unfolding is uniquely determined as

$$
\boxed{w_0 : w_1 : w_2 = 6 : 2 : 1}.
$$

*Proof.* The proof proceeds in three steps, each with clear mathematical justification.

**Step 1: Identify the three-level normal subgroup chain.**

The complete set of normal subgroups of $S_3$ is $\{e\}$, $A_3$, and $S_3$ (proven in §0.1.2). Hence the unique non-trivial normal subgroup chain is

$$
\{e\} \lhd A_3 \lhd S_3.
$$

The subgroups for the three levels are:
- $H_0 = \{e\}$ (finest level, trivial subgroup)
- $H_1 = A_3$ (intermediate level, alternating subgroup)
- $H_2 = S_3$ (coarsest level, full group)

This is the only possible unfolding path—not a human choice, but forced by the group-theoretic structure of $S_3$.

**Step 2: Compute the index of each level.**

By Lagrange's Theorem: for a finite group $G$ and a subgroup $H$, the index is

$$
[G : H] = \frac{|G|}{|H|}.
$$

Compute level by level:

| Level $i$ | Subgroup $H_i$ | $|H_i|$ | $|S_3|/|H_i|$ | Index $[S_3:H_i]$ |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| 0 | $\{e\}$ | $1$ | $6/1$ | 6 |
| 1 | $A_3$ | $3$ | $6/3$ | 2 |
| 2 | $S_3$ | $6$ | $6/6$ | 1 |

**Step 3: Obtain the ratio from the definition of spectral weight.**

By GT-0.1.0.8, the spectral weight $w_i = [S_3 : H_i]$. Therefore

$$
w_0 = [S_3 : \{e\}] = 6,
$$
$$
w_1 = [S_3 : A_3] = 2,
$$
$$
w_2 = [S_3 : S_3] = 1.
$$

All of the above computations hold exactly within the integers. Hence the spectral weight ratio is

$$
w_0 : w_1 : w_2 = 6 : 2 : 1.
$$

∎

**Structural commentary on GT-0.1.0.9:**

The mathematical meaning of these three weights is:

- **$w_0 = 6$** (maximal unfolding level): corresponds to the trivial subgroup $\{e\}$. At this level all group elements are distinguished; the spectral hierarchy space has $6$ independent directions. This is the finest resolution, carrying the richest spectral structure.

- **$w_1 = 2$** (intermediate level): corresponds to the alternating subgroup $A_3$. The even-odd dichotomy collapses 6 elements into 2 cosets; the spectral hierarchy space has $2$ independent directions (even domain and odd domain).

- **$w_2 = 1$** (ground state level): corresponds to the full group $S_3$ itself. No differentiation whatsoever; the spectral hierarchy space has only $1$ direction—the uniform background.
