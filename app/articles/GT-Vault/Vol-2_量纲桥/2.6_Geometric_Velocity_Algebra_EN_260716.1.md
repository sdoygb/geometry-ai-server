## §2.6.2 Velocity Structure in the Spectral Triple

### 2.6.2.1 The Commutator as Velocity Operator

In the Spectral Triple $(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{H}, D, J, \gamma)$, the Dirac operator $D$ plays the role of "differentiation." **Four-Equation Simultaneous System (GT-2.6.0.1)**

$$\boxed{v_f = i[D, f]}$$

where the factor $i$ ensures that $v_f$ is self-adjoint (since $D$ is self-adjoint, $f$ as a multiplication operator is self-adjoint, and $[D, f]$ is anti-self-adjoint).

**Boundedness of the velocity operator (GT-2.6.0.6).** For $f \in \mathcal{A}$, $v_f$ is a bounded operator:

$$\|v_f\|_{\mathcal{B}(\mathcal{H})} \leq \|df\|_{\infty}$$

where $\|df\|_{\infty}$ is the Lipschitz seminorm of $f$ under the metric defined by $D$. On $M(a) = S^3 \times S^3 \times S^3$, the spectrum of $v_f$ is governed by the spectral gap of $D$.

*Proof*: For any $\psi \in \mathcal{H}$, $\|[D, f]\psi\| = \|Df\psi - fD\psi\| \leq \|[D, f]\|_{\text{op}}\|\psi\|$. Moreover, $\|[D, f]\|_{\text{op}}$ equals the Lipschitz constant of $f$ under the Carnot–Carathéodory metric defined by $D$. On a compact manifold this constant is finite. ∎
