## Abstract

Within the foundational framework of Geometric Theory, this paper proceeds from the pure geometric structure of the Trifurcated Tangent Bundle $T\Sigma = \mathcal{M} \oplus \mathcal{C} \oplus \mathcal{I}$, obtained from the reduction of the $\mathrm{Cl}(9)$ spinor bundle of the nine-dimensional spectral triple $M(a)=S^3\times S^3\times S^3$, and establishes rigorous results in sub-bundle combinatorics. Core results: (i) The nonempty combination count of the three nontrivial sub-bundles of the Trifurcated Tangent Bundle is $2^3-1=7$, rigorously determined by combinatorics; this combination count provides heuristic consistency with the Seven-Layer Truncation Research Framework 2.1 of 0.2.2 ($N_{\mathrm{eff}}\le 7$, a combinatorial–K-theory research hypothesis), and together with the conjecture-level numerical correspondence with the Mersenne configuration number $2^7-1=127$ of 0.3.1.1 (deviation approximately 0.087%) constitutes a number-theoretic exploration direction awaiting rigorous formalization; (ii) Under the three-axiom constraint of the Completeness Axiom $\theta_M+\theta_C+\theta_I=90^\circ$ (0.0.6 Axiom 3), the six-term action $S(\theta)=\sum_{i}1/\sin^2\theta_i+\sum_{i<j}1/(\sin\theta_i\sin\theta_j)$, and the mass mapping $m_e=K\sin^3\theta_M$, the independent geometric variables are reduced from 3 to 2 dimensions; (iii) On the $\eta=0$ symmetric submanifold ($\theta_M=\theta_C$), the independent variables are further reduced to 1 dimension, the nonempty combination orbit count on this submanifold is 5, and the $\{\mathcal{M},\mathcal{C}\}$ orbit corresponds to the single core mapping layer of M-C Waist-Edge Coupling → electromagnetic interaction. This paper introduces no dynamical hypotheses, external source concepts, or scaling-law axioms; Theorems 1.1/1.2/2.1/2.2 are all rigorous derivations from pure combinatorics and geometric constraints, and provide the combinatorial-structural foundation for the spectral graph-theoretic tools of the 0.4.1 Information Field Decay Theorem.

## Chapter 1 Substructure of the Trifurcated Tangent Bundle

### §1.1 Nonempty Sub-bundle Combination Count

**Theorem 1.1 (Nonempty Sub-bundle Combination Count)**. The nonempty sub-bundle combination count of the Trifurcated Tangent Bundle $T\Sigma = \mathcal{M} \oplus \mathcal{C} \oplus \mathcal{I}$ is 7.

**Proof**. $T\Sigma$ consists of 3 nontrivial sub-bundles $\mathcal{M}$, $\mathcal{C}$, $\mathcal{I}$. Each sub-bundle can either appear or not appear in a combination, giving a total combination count of $2^3 = 8$. Excluding the empty set (none appearing), the nonempty combination count is $2^3 - 1 = 7$. ∎

The 7 nonempty sub-bundle combinations are:

| Nonempty Sub-bundle Combination | Combination Dimension | Series Physical Correspondence |
|:---:|:---:|:---|
| {𝓜} | 1 | Material Field source mode (electron mass sector) |
| {𝓒} | 1 | Causal Field curvature sector |
| {𝓘} | 1 | Information Field phase channel |
| {𝓜, 𝓒} | 2 | M-C Waist-Edge Coupling → electromagnetic interaction (single core mapping layer) |
| {𝓒, 𝓘} | 2 | Causal–Information joint sector |
| {𝓘, 𝓜} | 2 | Information–Material joint sector |
| {𝓜, 𝓒, 𝓘} | 3 | Complete Trifurcated Tangent Bundle (full theory) |

**Note 1.1 (Heuristic association with Seven-Layer Truncation and Mersenne configuration number)**. The combination count $7=2^3-1$ is heuristically consistent with the combinatorial interpretation of the Seven-Layer Truncation Research Framework 2.1 of 0.2.2 ($N_{\mathrm{eff}}\le 7$, a combinatorial–K-theory research hypothesis). Caution: that the combination count equals the number of independent encoding channels is a research hypothesis, not a consequence of the three axioms (0.2.2 §2.3.1). The combinatorial completeness of the Trifurcated Tangent Bundle $(2^3-1=7)$ coincides at the group-theoretic level with K-theory Bott Periodicity ($\mathrm{Cl}_{0,6}\cong\mathbb{R}(8)$ providing an 8-dimensional real irreducible representation, $\mathrm{Cl}_{0,7}\cong\mathbb{C}(8)$ as the complexification boundary, $\mathrm{Cl}_{0,8}\cong\mathbb{R}(16)$ periodic return), offering structural heuristic support for an upper bound on independent encoding channels.

Furthermore, the Mersenne configuration number $2^7-1=127$ numerically corresponds to $\Lambda\cdot\sin\theta_M^0\approx 127.110$ in 0.3.1.1 ($\sin\theta_M^0=0.8474000449$, $\Lambda=150$ as the structural identification of the Hessian Soft-to-Hard Mode ratio, deviation approximately $0.087\%$). Caution: the $\Lambda=150$ here differs in meaning and numerical value from the spectral proportionality parameter $\Lambda=3$ used in the combinatorics of this paper. This numerical correspondence is a preliminary observation at the level of 0.3.1.1 Conjecture 4.1 (Mersenne configuration number); a rigorous algebraic derivation has not yet been established.

**Theorem 1.2 (Dimensional Constraint)**. ### §2.1 The $\eta = 0$ Submanifold

In the intrinsic coordinates $(\xi, \eta)$ of 0.4.1, $\xi=\theta_I$ (Information Field angle), $\eta=\theta_C-\theta_M$ (Causal–Material angle difference). The submanifold $\eta = 0$ corresponds to $\theta_M = \theta_C$ (Causal–Material symmetry).

**Theorem 2.1 (Dimension of the Symmetric Submanifold)**. The $\eta = 0$ submanifold is a 1-dimensional curve on the constraint section $\Sigma$.

**Proof**. On $\Sigma$, the Completeness Axiom has already removed 1 dimension. Imposing $\theta_M = \theta_C$ (i.e., $\eta = 0$) adds 1 independent constraint, reducing the degrees of freedom from 2 to 1. Hence $\eta = 0$ is a 1-dimensional submanifold on $\Sigma$. ∎

The $\eta=0$ submanifold contains the projection of the background fixed point $P_0=(30^\circ,0)$ along the $\eta$ direction, corresponding to the symmetric configuration $\theta_M=\theta_C=\theta_I=30^\circ$. The physical realization point $P_e=(5.91^\circ,-31.77^\circ)$ deviates from this submanifold, reflecting the breaking of Material–Causal symmetry.

- $\{\mathcal{M}, \mathcal{C}, \mathcal{I}\}$ is invariant, forming 1 orbit of size 1

Total orbit count = $1 + 1 + 1 + 1 + 1 = 5$. Verified by Burnside's Lemma: $Z_2$ has 2 elements; the identity fixes 7 combinations, the exchange fixes 3 combinations ($\{\mathcal{I}\}$, $\{\mathcal{M}, \mathcal{C}\}$, $\{\mathcal{M}, \mathcal{C}, \mathcal{I}\}$); orbit count = $(7 + 3)/2 = 5$. ∎
