## Abstract

Within the foundational framework of Geometric Theory, this paper employs random matrix spectral theory and spectral graph-theoretic decay theorems to establish a rigorous mathematical formulation of information field decay. Core results: intrinsic coordinates $(\xi, \eta)$ are established on the completeness axiom constraint section $\Sigma$, restricting the Six-Term Action to the potential function $V = S|_\Sigma$; the information field decay rate is defined via the Hessian metric $g_{ij} = \partial_i \partial_j V$, with decay modes given by the fundamental-frequency coordinates of graph Laplacian eigenfunctions. The core of the Decay Theorem (Theorem 3.1) rests upon the rigorous spectral graph-theoretic framework of 0.4: the algebraic connectivity $\mu_1$ and the characteristic decay rate $\Gamma_{\text{geo}}$ satisfy $\Gamma_{\text{geo}} = \mu_1/N_{\text{info}}$ (0.4 Theorem 7.1, rigorous part), a relation that does not depend on any conjectures. The numerical values $\Gamma_{\text{geo}} = 5.75 \times 10^{-23}$ and $N_{\text{dec}} = 1.74 \times 10^{22}$ are self-consistent with observation under the premise that 0.4 Conjecture 4.2 (the combinatorial formula for $C_{\text{geo}}$) and Conjecture 5.1 (the $N_{\text{dec}}$ scaling hypothesis) hold. This paper honestly labels all conjecture dependencies, explicitly distinguishing the rigorous parts of the theorem from its conditional parts. No dynamical equations or evolutionary hypotheses are introduced; all content is purely geometric definition and spectral analysis.

**Keywords:** Decay Theorem; random matrix; spectral graph theory; graph Laplacian; Cheeger inequality; heat kernel decay; Fiedler vector; Dimensional Bridge; Nine-Dimensional Spectral Triple

$$\frac{\partial \rho}{\partial \sigma} = -L_G \rho$$

**Theorem 3.1 (Decay Theorem — Rigorous Part)**. For arbitrary initial data $\rho_0 \in \ell^2(V)$, the solution of the graph heat equation satisfies exponential decay:
$$\|\rho(\sigma) - \rho_{\text{eq}}\|_{\ell^2} \leq C \cdot e^{-\sigma \Gamma_{\text{geo}}} \|\rho_0 - \rho_{\text{eq}}\|_{\ell^2}$$

where the characteristic decay rate is rigorously determined by the algebraic connectivity $\mu_1$ of the holographic screen graph $G$:
$$\Gamma_{\text{geo}} = \frac{\mu_1}{N_{\text{info}}}$$

**Proof**. By standard results of spectral graph theory, the decay rate of the graph heat kernel is determined by the spectral gap $\mu_1$ (algebraic connectivity). $\Gamma_{\text{geo}} = \mu_1/N_{\text{info}}$ is the normalized decay rate per information vertex, rigorously established in the theory of graph Laplacian semigroups (0.4 Theorem 6.1 guarantees existence and uniqueness of the semigroup; Theorem 7.1 provides the relation between $\mu_1$ and $\Gamma_{\text{geo}}$). $N_{\text{info}}$ is derived from 0.2.2. ### §3.2 Cheeger Inequality and Algebraic Connectivity

The algebraic connectivity $\mu_1$ (Fiedler value) of the graph Laplacian is related to the geometric decay rate by the Cheeger inequality:

$$\frac{h_G^2}{2} \leq \mu_1 \leq 2h_G$$

where $h_G$ is the Cheeger constant (isoperimetric constant) of the graph, characterizing the bottleneck of the holographic screen graph.

**Theorem 3.2**. The algebraic connectivity and geometric decay rate of the holographic screen graph $G$ satisfy:
$$\Gamma_{\text{geo}} = \frac{\mu_1}{N_{\text{info}}}$$

This relation is rigorously established in spectral graph theory. Numerically: under the premise that 0.4 Conjecture 4.2 and Conjecture 5.1 hold, $\mu_1 = 5.18$, $N_{\text{info}} = 9.024 \times 10^{22}$, $\Gamma_{\text{geo}} = 5.75 \times 10^{-23}$.

From the left half of the Cheeger inequality $h_G^2/2 \leq \mu_1$ we obtain $h_G \geq \sqrt{2\mu_1} = 3.22$; from the right half $\mu_1 \leq 2h_G$ we obtain $h_G \geq \mu_1/2 = 2.59$.
